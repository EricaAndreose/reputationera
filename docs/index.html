A popularity journey between De Kooning and Klimt

Explore artists' popularity

Check the relationship between the popularity of an artist in the economic dimension of the art market and in the cultural dimension of the general public.


Is there a correlation between the sales of an artist's works and his popularity in terms of exhibitions and engangment?

We will consider two case studies: Willem De Kooning and Gustav Klimt.

1862 - 1918

1904 - 1997



We selected these two artists for various reasons:

  • Quantitative similarity in auction data, enabling us to conduct a balanced comparison.
  • Similar time coverage of the data period.
  • Possibility of using De Kooning as a reference for evaluating the completeness of data and research results.
  • Observation of Klimt as a fresh case study and for final comparison between the two artists.



The sources of our data:

De Kooning Foundation

A complete digital resource with everything about Willem De Kooning.
Blouin Art Sales Index

Exaustive database with art auction records (since 1922) from over 350 auction houses.
BnF and Google Books

Open dataset for the bibliographic records exploration and extraction.
Wikidata Sparql endopoint

Open service to query Wikidata information to enlarge our artists' dataset.

Willem De Kooning


How much can we trust our data and our results?

We initiated our analysis with Willem de Kooning upon encountering the online portal of the foundation dedicated to the artist. This website encompasses comprehensive information regarding the artist's works, sales, exhibitions, bibliographies, and more.
Recognizing it as a rich and authoritative resource, we leveraged it to gauge the extent of completeness in the available open data, which we subsequently applied to the study of the second artist in our research. Consequently, we utilized the foundation's website as a benchmark dataset to assess the completeness of the auction data, bibliographies, and exhibition records at our disposal.
